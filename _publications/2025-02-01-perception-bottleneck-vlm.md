# Abstract

Charts are ubiquitous data visualization tools that convey complex quantitative information efficiently. While vision-language models (VLMs) have shown progress on various visual tasks, chart understanding remains a significant challenge. We investigate the perception bottleneck of VLMs for chart understanding through a systematic analysis. We find that current VLMs struggle with fine-grained chart element recognition, data value extraction, and structural understanding of charts. We propose a focused analysis framework that isolates the perception and reasoning components of chart understanding, revealing the specific bottlenecks in current VLMs. Based on our analysis, we identify key directions for improving chart understanding in VLMs.
