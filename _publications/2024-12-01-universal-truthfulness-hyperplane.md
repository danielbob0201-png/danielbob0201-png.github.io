# Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) are known to generate false information, or so-called "hallucinations," which poses significant challenges for their deployment in real-world applications. A fundamental question in understanding and mitigating LLM hallucinations is whether there exists a universal mechanism underlying model truthfulness. In this work, we discover a universal truthfulness hyperplane inside LLMs. We find that representations of truthful and hallucinated responses are linearly separable by a direction that is consistent across different LLMs and even transferable between models. This hyperplane exists at different layers of the model and is related to the model's internal truthfulness mechanism.
