# Abstract

Evaluating large language models (LLMs) is crucial for tracking their progress and understanding their limitations. While existing benchmarks primarily focus on English and general-purpose tasks, a comprehensive evaluation of LLMs in the Chinese language context remains underexplored. We present C-Eval, a multi-level multi-discipline Chinese evaluation suite for assessing the advanced knowledge and reasoning abilities of foundation models in the Chinese language. C-Eval consists of 13,948 multiple-choice questions spanning 52 disciplines from elementary to professional levels, with three difficulty tiers. Evaluating nine representative LLMs on C-Eval reveals that current models still struggle significantly with the professional-level questions, highlighting the need for further advancements.
