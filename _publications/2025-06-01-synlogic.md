# Abstract

Logical reasoning is a fundamental capacity for large language models (LLMs), yet training LLMs for complex logical reasoning remains challenging due to the scarcity of high-quality, verifiable reasoning data. We present SynLogic, a framework for synthesizing large-scale, verifiable logical reasoning data. SynLogic systematically generates diverse reasoning chains across multiple logical domains, with built-in verifiability that eliminates the need for human annotation of correctness labels. We show that models trained on SynLogic data achieve substantial improvements in logical reasoning benchmarks across multiple domains. Compared to existing synthetic data generation methods, SynLogic produces higher-quality data with better coverage and verifiability.
